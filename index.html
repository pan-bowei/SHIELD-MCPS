Why this matters

Prepare-pulse-drop drops PRE-PREPARE to f+1 replicas, preventing quorum and forcing a view change. It is the simplest attack: fixed and fully known, isolating the leader-selection rule as the variable under test.

About this demo

This page is an interactive illustration of the FSM-MCPS and SHIELD-MCPS primary-selection mechanisms: Algorithm 1/2 selection logic, the seven exploit classes (E1–E7), and SHIELD-MCPS's five structural fixes plus DQN-adaptive cooldown κ*(v). It runs at small scale (n=10–50) for real-time visual clarity. The numbers shown here are not the paper's reported evaluation results — those use n=50–200 over thousands of rounds with the full reputation dynamics (see Table I, Fig. 4–7, and the fairness heatmap in the paper). This demo is for building intuition about why and how the mechanisms work; the paper reports what happens at evaluation scale.
